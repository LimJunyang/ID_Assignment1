Oasis, formed in Manchester in 1991, became one of the most successful English rock bands. Initially composed of Liam Gallagher, Paul Arthurs, Paul McGuigan, and Tony McCarroll, Noel Gallagher later joined. Known for their debut album "Definitely Maybe" (1994) and the iconic "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" (1995), Oasis set records and achieved massive success. The band faced internal conflicts, with the Gallagher brothers often making headlines for their disputes. Despite lineup changes, Oasis released several more albums before disbanding in 2009. As of 2023, they have sold over 75 million records worldwide, earned numerous awards, and left a lasting impact on the music industry.
